About me

Linux user since 1996, I discovered the GIS world in 2005 reading an article about GRASS GIS and environmental applications. Starting with vector and raster analysis, I moved to web technologies and then, thanks to PyWPS, I could finally combine the two worlds. This became the main topic for my MD Thesis (2007).

Since that time I have dealt with different activities: webgis applications, web tools for report generation, data analysis, geodatabase, etc.. In every tool that I develop, my goal is always the same: allow all types of users to benefit from the potential of GIS technologies. I am not a supporter of "maps at all costs": I think it's more important to provide the user with the right tools to quickly and efficiently find the needed informations and then give the ability to view the results on a map. I love teaching because it's the best way to really understand problems.
I am a member of the GRASS-GFOSS Meeting Scientific Committee since 2009 and I have a been a Board member of the Italian Geographic Free/Open-Source Software Association from 2008 to 2010. I enjoy boardgames (my collection), books and swimming.
